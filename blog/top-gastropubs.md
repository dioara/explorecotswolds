# The Foodie's Guide to the Cotswolds: 5 Must-Visit Gastropubs

**Published on:** December 8, 2025 | **Author:** Manus AI | **Category:** Eat & Drink

![Cotswolds Pub](/images/restaurants/wild-rabbit.jpg)

The Cotswolds is not only a feast for the eyes but also for the palate. The region is home to a fantastic array of traditional pubs and inns, many of which have been transformed into award-winning gastropubs. Here are five of the best gastropubs in the Cotswolds, where you can enjoy delicious food in a cozy and welcoming atmosphere.

## 1. The Wild Rabbit, Kingham

Often referred to as "the poshest pub in Britain," **The Wild Rabbit** in Kingham is a stylish and sophisticated gastropub with a focus on modern British cuisine. The pub has its own organic farm, which supplies much of the produce used in the kitchen. The menu changes with the seasons, but you can always expect to find creative and beautifully presented dishes.

## 2. The Kingham Plough, Kingham

Also in the village of Kingham, **The Kingham Plough** is a Michelin-starred gastropub that has been praised for its innovative and delicious food. The pub has a relaxed and informal atmosphere, and the menu features a mix of classic pub dishes and more refined creations. The Sunday lunch is particularly popular, so be sure to book in advance.

## 3. The Ebrington Arms, Ebrington

**The Ebrington Arms** is a charming 17th-century inn that has been named "the best village pub in the UK" on multiple occasions. The pub has a cozy and traditional interior, with open fires and exposed beams. The menu features a range of classic pub dishes, all made with locally sourced ingredients. The pub also has its own brewery, so you can enjoy a pint of their own ale with your meal.

## 4. The Feathered Nest, Nether Westcote

Perched on a hilltop with stunning views of the Evenlode Valley, **The Feathered Nest** is a luxury country inn with an award-winning restaurant. The menu features modern British cuisine with a focus on game and other local produce. The restaurant has a more formal atmosphere than some of the other pubs on this list, making it a great choice for a special occasion.

## 5. The Horse and Groom, Bourton-on-the-Hill

**The Horse and Groom** is a traditional village pub with a reputation for excellent food. The pub has a cozy and welcoming atmosphere, and the menu features a range of classic pub dishes, all made with fresh, local ingredients. The pub is a popular spot for walkers, as it is located on the Cotswold Way.
