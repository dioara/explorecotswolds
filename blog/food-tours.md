# A Culinary Journey Through the Cotswolds: 5 Foodie Tours and Experiences

**Published on:** December 8, 2025 | **Author:** Manus AI | **Category:** Tours

![Cotswolds Food Tour](/images/tours/food-tour.jpg)

The Cotswolds is a food lover's paradise, with a rich culinary heritage and a thriving food and drink scene. A food tour is a great way to discover the region's local produce, meet the artisans who create it, and sample some of the delicious food and drink on offer. Here are five of the best foodie tours and experiences in the Cotswolds.

## 1. Cotswolds Distillery Tour & Tasting

Located in the heart of the Cotswolds, the Cotswolds Distillery is an award-winning distillery that produces a range of spirits, including gin, whisky, and liqueurs. You can take a tour of the distillery to see how the spirits are made, and then enjoy a tasting of their products. The distillery also has a shop where you can buy their spirits to take home with you.

## 2. Daylesford Cookery School

Daylesford is one of the most famous farm shops in the country, and it also has a state-of-the-art cookery school. You can take a range of courses, from a half-day introduction to bread making to a full-day course on nose-to-tail butchery. The courses are all taught by expert chefs, and you'll get to use the best organic produce from the farm.

## 3. Huffkins Bakery School

If you've ever wanted to learn how to make the perfect scone, then a course at the Huffkins Bakery School is a must. This family-run bakery has been making delicious cakes and pastries since 1890, and they now offer a range of courses where you can learn their secrets. You can learn how to make everything from bread and pastries to cakes and afternoon tea.

## 4. Cotswold Foodie Tours

If you want to discover the best food and drink that the Cotswolds has to offer, then a Cotswold Foodie Tour is a great option. These guided tours take you to a range of different producers, from cheesemakers and brewers to chocolatiers and bakers. You'll get to sample their products, learn about how they are made, and meet the people behind them.

## 5. Farmers' Market Foraging

The Cotswolds is home to a number of excellent farmers' markets, and a great way to experience the local food scene is to go on a foraging trip. You can wander through the stalls, chat to the producers, and pick up a range of delicious local produce to create your own Cotswolds feast. Stroud Farmers' Market is one of the biggest and best, but you can also find excellent markets in Cirencester, Cheltenham, and Moreton-in-Marsh.
